# SOP-003: Incident Reporting Procedures
## Vanguard Elite Services Limited

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** TBD
**Approved By:** Roger Kumin, Director

---

## 1. Purpose

Establishes a standardized incident reporting process to ensure all security incidents are documented accurately, reported promptly, and escalated appropriately.

---

## 2. Incident Categories

| Category | Examples | Response Level |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| **CRITICAL** | Armed attack, serious injury, death, fire, major theft | Immediate: Police + Director + Client |
| **HIGH** | Break-in, assault, significant property damage, trespass with threat | Immediate: Supervisor + Client |
| **MEDIUM** | Minor theft, unauthorized access, minor property damage, suspicious activity | Within 1 hour: Supervisor |
| **LOW** | Equipment malfunction, minor policy violation, maintenance issue | End of shift: Supervisor |

---

## 3. Immediate Response Protocol

### All Incidents:
1. **SAFETY FIRST** -- Ensure your personal safety and the safety of others
2. **OBSERVE** -- Note time, location, persons, actions, descriptions
3. **REPORT** -- Notify supervisor/control immediately
4. **RECORD** -- Write in Occurrence Book immediately
5. **PRESERVE** -- Do not disturb the scene; protect evidence

### Critical Incidents Additional Steps:
6. **CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES** -- Police 112, Fire 110, Ambulance 111
7. **SECURE THE SCENE** -- Prevent access until authorities arrive
8. **DIRECTOR NOTIFICATION** -- Roger Kumin must be notified within 15 minutes
9. **CLIENT NOTIFICATION** -- Client site manager notified within 30 minutes

## 5. Occurrence Book (Daily Log)

Every guard post must maintain a bound Occurrence Book (OB). ALL events during a shift are recorded, including:

- Shift start/end times
- Guard names on duty
- Visitor entries/exits
- Patrol times and findings
- Weather conditions (if relevant)
- Equipment issues
- Incidents (with reference to Incident Report number)
- Instructions received
- Any other noteworthy observations

**Rules:**
- Write in blue or black ink only
- No pages to be torn out
- Errors to be crossed out with single line (still readable) and initialed
- Sequential entries with no gaps
- Supervisor to review and sign at least once per 24 hours

---

## 6. Report Distribution

| Incident Level | Reports To | Within |
|---------------|------------|--------|
| CRITICAL | Director + Client + Police + SIA (if required) | Immediately |
| HIGH | Supervisor + Client | 4 hours |
| MEDIUM | Supervisor | 12 hours |
| LOW | Supervisor | 24 hours |

---

## 7. Record Retention

- All Incident Reports: Retain for minimum 7 years
- Occurrence Books: Retain for minimum 3 years
- Store copies securely at registered office
- Provide copies to client as per contract requirements
