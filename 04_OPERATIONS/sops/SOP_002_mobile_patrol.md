**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Purpose

Establishes procedures for mobile patrol operations including perimeter checks, key point inspections, and roving security at client sites.

---

## 2. Patrol Types

| Type | Description | Frequency |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Scheduled Patrol | Fixed route at predetermined times | Per site instructions |
| Random Patrol | Varied route and timing to prevent predictability | Minimum 2 per shift |
| Response Patrol | Deployed in response to alarm, report, or request | As needed |

---

## 3. Patrol Procedures

### 3.1 Before Patrol
1. Log patrol start time in Occurrence Book
2. Carry torch, notebook, radio/phone, patrol checkpoint register
3. Inform control point/static guard of departure

### 3.2 During Patrol
1. Follow designated route (scheduled) or vary route (random)
2. Check all designated checkpoints -- sign/stamp patrol register at each point
3. Check perimeter fencing, gates, locks, lighting
4. Check for signs of intrusion, damage, fire hazards, water leaks
5. Challenge any unidentified persons -- identify and verify authorization
6. Report findings immediately if urgent, or at patrol completion if routine
7. Do NOT use shortcuts through unsafe areas
8. Maintain awareness of surroundings at all times

### 3.3 After Patrol
1. Log patrol completion time in Occurrence Book
2. Report all findings to supervisor:
   - Fencing/gate condition
   - Lighting status (working/not working)
   - Any unauthorized persons or vehicles observed
   - Any damage, hazards, or unusual activity
   - Any maintenance issues noted
3. Complete Patrol Report Form

---

## 4. Night Patrol Additional Procedures

1. Always carry a functional torch with spare batteries
2. Move quietly and use natural cover where appropriate
3. Allow eyes to adjust to darkness before starting patrol
4. Check dark areas, blind spots, and hidden recesses
5. Do NOT patrol alone in high-risk areas if possible
6. Maintain radio/phone contact with control point at all times
7. Report check-in at predetermined intervals (every 30 minutes minimum)
