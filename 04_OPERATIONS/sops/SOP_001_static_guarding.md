## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the duties, responsibilities, and conduct standards for all Vanguard Elite Services security officers assigned to static guarding duties.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all security officers performing static guarding at any client site, including but not limited to:
- Gate/access control points
- Guard houses and control rooms
- Perimeter posts
- Building reception and lobby security

---

## 3. General Duties

### 3.1 Shift Commencement
1. Report to site at least 15 minutes before shift start
2. Sign in on the Guard Attendance Register
3. Receive handover briefing from outgoing guard (see Section 5)
4. Inspect post and surrounding area
5. Check all equipment is present and functional:
   - Torch/flashlight
   - Notebook and pen
   - Guard ID card displayed
   - Communication device (if issued)
   - Keys (if applicable)
6. Read and acknowledge any new instructions in the Site Order Book

### 3.2 During Shift
1. Remain at assigned post at all times unless authorized to leave
2. Conduct regular visual checks of assigned area
3. Monitor and control access per site-specific instructions
4. Record all visitors, vehicles, and deliveries in the Visitor/Vehicle Log
5. Report any incidents, suspicious activity, or unusual occurrences immediately
6. Maintain professional appearance and conduct at all times
7. Do NOT sleep, consume alcohol, or use mobile phones for personal use while on duty
8. Do NOT leave post unattended -- request relief through supervisor

### 3.3 Shift Handover
1. Brief incoming guard on:
   - Current status of site
   - Any ongoing incidents or issues
   - Pending tasks or instructions
   - Equipment status
   - Key holder information
2. Complete and sign the Shift Handover Form
3. Do NOT leave post until incoming guard is present and briefed

### 3.4 Shift End
1. Complete all log entries for the shift
2. Return all equipment to proper storage (if end of day)
3. Sign out on the Guard Attendance Register
4. Report any outstanding issues to supervisor

---

## 4. Access Control Procedures

### 4.1 Personnel Access
1. All persons entering the site must be identified and authorized
2. Check identification against authorized personnel list
3. Record visitor name, company, purpose, time in/out in Visitor Log
4. Issue visitor pass/badge if applicable
5. Escort unauthorized persons off premises politely but firmly
6. Deny entry to intoxicated or aggressive persons -- notify supervisor

### 4.2 Vehicle Access
1. All vehicles must stop at the gate/checkpoint
2. Verify driver identity and authorization
3. Record vehicle registration, driver name, time in/out
4. Inspect vehicles if required by site-specific instructions
5. Direct vehicles to designated parking areas

### 4.3 Deliveries
1. Verify delivery against expected delivery schedule (if available)
2. Record delivery company, driver, vehicle registration, goods description
3. Contact site manager/client representative to authorize receipt
4. Do NOT sign for goods unless authorized to do so

---

## 5. Shift Handover Protocol

The outgoing guard must verbally brief AND complete the written Shift Handover Form covering:

| Item | Detail Required |
|------|----------------|
| Date and Time | Handover date, outgoing shift end time, incoming shift start time |
| Outgoing Guard | Name and signature |
| Incoming Guard | Name and signature |
| Site Status | Normal / Incident in progress / Alert |
| Incidents | Any incidents during shift -- reference Occurrence Book entries |
| Equipment | Status of all equipment (working/damaged/missing) |
| Keys | Number and identification of keys handed over |
| Pending Actions | Tasks that need follow-up |
| Special Instructions | Any temporary orders or client instructions |

---

## 6. Incident Reporting

### 6.1 Immediate Actions
For ANY incident (theft, trespass, damage, injury, fire, etc.):
1. Ensure personal safety first
2. Call for assistance if needed (supervisor, emergency services)
3. Do NOT confront armed persons -- observe and report
4. Preserve the scene where possible
5. Note time, location, persons involved, descriptions
6. Notify supervisor immediately by phone/radio

### 6.2 Written Report
Complete an Incident Report Form within 2 hours of incident:
- Date, time, location
- Description of what happened (factual, not opinion)
- Persons involved (names, descriptions if unknown)
- Actions taken
- Witnesses
- Guard's signature

### 6.3 Escalation
- **Theft/break-in:** Notify client + supervisor + police
- **Injury:** First aid + supervisor + ambulance if needed
- **Fire:** Evacuate + fire brigade + supervisor + client
- **Trespass:** Challenge verbally + supervisor + police if needed
- **Suspicious package:** Do NOT touch + evacuate area + supervisor + police

## 7. Code of Conduct

All Vanguard Elite Services security officers must:

1. **Be professional** -- Uniform clean and complete, ID visible, courteous manner
2. **Be honest** -- No theft, fraud, or corruption of any kind (immediate dismissal)
3. **Be alert** -- No sleeping, no alcohol/drugs, no unauthorized absences
4. **Be respectful** -- Treat all persons with dignity regardless of background
5. **Use minimum force** -- Physical force is an absolute last resort for self-defense only
6. **Maintain confidentiality** -- Client information is strictly confidential
7. **Follow the chain of command** -- Report through supervisor, not directly to client management (unless emergency)
8. **Respect community** -- Maintain positive relationships with local communities (critical in forestry/rural operations)

---

## 8. Prohibited Actions

The following will result in disciplinary action up to and including immediate dismissal:

- Sleeping on duty
- Consuming alcohol or drugs before or during shift
- Theft or dishonesty of any kind
- Unauthorized use of force
- Abandoning post without authorization
- Using client facilities without permission
- Disclosing confidential information
- Carrying unauthorized weapons
- Intimidating, harassing, or threatening any person
- Accepting gifts or bribes
