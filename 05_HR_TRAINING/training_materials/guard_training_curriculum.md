# Guard Training Curriculum -- Vanguard Elite Services Limited
## Class A (Unarmed) Security Officer Training Program

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-01-30
**Duration:** 5 days (40 hours) initial training + ongoing professional development

---

## Purpose

This training program satisfies SIA requirements for guard training and ensures all Vanguard Elite Services security officers are competent, professional, and prepared for deployment. This curriculum must be submitted as part of the SIA license application.

---

## DAY 1: Foundations (8 hours)

### Module 1.1: Introduction to the Security Industry (2 hrs)
- Overview of the PNG security industry
- Role of the Security Industries Authority (SIA)
- Security Protection Act 2004 -- key provisions
- Guard rights and responsibilities under the law
- Consequences of unlawful conduct (Sections 50, 51 penalties)

### Module 1.2: Vanguard Elite Services Company Induction (2 hrs)
- Company mission, values, and standards
- Organizational structure and chain of command
- Employee rights and obligations
- Employment contract overview
- Code of Conduct (detailed review and sign-off)
- Disciplinary procedures

### Module 1.3: Legal Powers and Limitations (2 hrs)
- Powers of a security officer (observation, reporting, access control)
- What security officers CANNOT do (arrest powers, use of force limitations)
- Citizen's arrest provisions and when they apply
- Trespass laws and procedures
- Liability and legal consequences of exceeding authority

### Module 1.4: Professional Conduct and Appearance (2 hrs)
- Uniform standards and grooming
- Communication skills -- verbal and non-verbal
- Customer service and public relations
- Dealing with difficult people
- Cultural sensitivity and community relations (wantok system, landowner issues)
- Anti-corruption and integrity standards

---

## DAY 2: Core Security Operations (8 hours)

### Module 2.1: Static Guarding (SOP-001) (3 hrs)
- Post duties and responsibilities
- Shift handover procedures
- Access control procedures (personnel, vehicles, deliveries)
- Key control and management
- Occurrence Book maintenance
- Practical exercise: Simulated shift handover

### Module 2.2: Mobile Patrol (SOP-002) (2 hrs)
- Patrol types and purposes
- Route planning and variation
- Checkpoint procedures
- Night patrol techniques
- Observation and awareness skills
- Practical exercise: Simulated patrol with reporting

### Module 2.3: Incident Reporting (SOP-003) (3 hrs)
- Incident categories and response levels
- Immediate response protocol
- Written report writing (clear, factual, complete)
- Occurrence Book entries
- Evidence preservation basics
- Practical exercise: Write incident reports from scenarios

---

## DAY 3: Safety and Emergency Response (8 hours)

### Module 3.1: Occupational Health and Safety (2 hrs)
- Workplace hazards for security officers
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Heat stress and hydration (tropical climate)
- Fatigue management for shift workers
- Reporting unsafe conditions

### Module 3.2: First Aid (4 hrs)
- Scene assessment and safety
- CPR basics (adult)
- Bleeding control (direct pressure, elevation)
- Shock management
- Burns treatment
- Snake bite response (common in PNG bush/forestry sites)
- Medical emergency response
- When to call for ambulance/medical assistance
- Practical exercise: First aid scenarios

### Module 3.3: Fire Safety (2 hrs)
- Fire triangle and types of fire
- Fire extinguisher types and use (PASS technique)
- Evacuation procedures
- Fire reporting and alarm activation
- Practical exercise: Fire extinguisher use (if equipment available)

---

## DAY 4: Site-Specific and Sector Training (8 hours)

### Module 4.1: Forestry Sector Security (3 hrs)
- Overview of forestry operations and common security threats
- Log theft and timber fraud awareness
- Equipment and machinery protection
- Remote site operations -- unique challenges
- Community and landowner engagement
- Environmental compliance awareness (logging permits, boundaries)
- Wildlife and natural hazard awareness

### Module 4.2: Conflict Resolution and De-escalation (3 hrs)
- Understanding conflict triggers
- Verbal de-escalation techniques
- Body language and positioning
- When to stand ground vs. when to withdraw
- Community disputes in forestry areas
- Role play exercises: De-escalation scenarios

### Module 4.3: Communication and Reporting (2 hrs)
- Radio/phone communication protocols
- Phonetic alphabet
- Situation reports (SITREP format)
- Briefing and debriefing procedures
- Report writing practice

---

## DAY 5: Assessment and Deployment Preparation (8 hours)

### Module 5.1: Written Assessment (2 hrs)
- Multiple choice and short answer test covering:
  - Legal framework
  - SOPs
  - Incident reporting
  - First aid
  - Fire safety
  - Code of conduct
- **Pass mark: 70%**

### Module 5.2: Practical Assessment (3 hrs)
- Simulated shift scenario:
  - Shift handover
  - Access control decisions
  - Incident response
  - Report writing
  - Communication
- Assessed by Director or Training Supervisor

### Module 5.3: Deployment Briefing (2 hrs)
- Site-specific briefing for assigned location
- Client expectations and specific instructions
- Travel and logistics arrangements
- Emergency contact information
- Equipment and uniform issue
- Questions and clarification

### Module 5.4: Administration (1 hr)
- Training certificate issuance
- Guard profile form completion (SIA requirement)
- Uniform and equipment issue (sign for receipt)
- Contract signing (if not already done)
- Payroll setup

## Ongoing Professional Development

| Training | Frequency | Duration |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Refresher training (SOPs, legal) | Every 6 months | 4 hours |
| First aid recertification | Annually | 4 hours |
| Site-specific updates | As needed | 1-2 hours |
| New SOP training | When SOPs updated | 1-2 hours |
| Performance review | Quarterly | 1 hour |

---

## Training Records

For each guard, maintain:
1. Training attendance register (signed)
2. Assessment results (written + practical)
3. Training certificate copy
4. Ongoing development records
5. Disciplinary/performance records

**Retain all training records for the duration of employment + 3 years.**
