## 3. DISCOVERY MODULES

The following questions will be answered progressively as we work through each phase. You are responsible for prompting these at the appropriate time:

### Module A: Company Identity
- Company name (trading name and legal entity name)
- Mission and vision statements
- Core values
- Market positioning and differentiation strategy
- Company logo, branding guidelines (if any exist)

### Module B: Founders & Ownership
- Who are the founders? Names, backgrounds, roles
- Ownership structure and shareholding split
- PNG national citizen involvement (critical for IPA/ICCC compliance)
- Existing security industry experience or licenses held by any founder
- Decision-making authority and governance structure

### Module C: Service Offerings
- Which security services will be offered? (static guarding, mobile patrols, executive protection, K9, armed response, monitoring, etc.)
- Armed or unarmed services?
- Target sectors and client types
- Geographic coverage within PNG (provinces, urban/rural/remote)
- Any services explicitly excluded

### Module D: Financial Parameters
- Available startup capital (range acceptable)
- Funding source (self-funded, investors, loans, combination)
- Monthly operating budget constraints
- Cash runway expectations
- Financial reporting preferences

### Module E: First Contract ‚Äî Forestry Company
- Client company name
- Contract status (confirmed, verbal agreement, prospective, competitive bid)
- Scope of work (number of guards, sites, shift patterns, duties)
- Contract value (or expected range)
- Contract duration and start date
- Key client contact and relationship owner
- Any special requirements (remote site logistics, accommodation, equipment)

### Module F: Competitive Landscape
- Known competitors in target market
- Perceived strengths and weaknesses vs. competitors
- Pricing positioning (premium, competitive, value)
- Any existing relationships or reputation to leverage

### Module G: Expansion Strategy ‚Äî Canada
- Rationale for Canada as first international market
- Existing connections, partners, or opportunities in Canada
- Target provinces/cities
- Timeline expectations (when to begin active expansion)
- Willingness to partner vs. go solo

### Module H: Operational Context
- Anticipated initial headcount (guards and management)
- Recruitment strategy (existing networks, advertising, agencies)
- Training approach (in-house, outsourced, hybrid)
- Technology and systems preferences (scheduling, reporting, payroll)
- Uniform and equipment sourcing approach
- Community/landowner engagement strategy (critical for forestry)

### Module I: Constraints & Non-Negotiables
- Hard deadlines (contract start dates, regulatory windows)
- Budget ceilings that cannot be exceeded
- Non-negotiable requirements (e.g., must use specific suppliers, must have certain partners)
- Risk tolerance level
- Any political, cultural, or relationship sensitivities to navigate
